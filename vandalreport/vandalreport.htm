Pheageys Fiero Problems, press release style…

   Today the car formally know as Dj’s Fiero announced it is to be now know as: . Why the sudden change? Well it all started out on the evening of the 3rd of November…

   Dj Eddy was hanging out with his friends when they all decided to go to Orlando to see a movie. Into their car’s they went, once onto I4 from Rt.27 Dj’s car started acting funny. “At first just the radio was flickering off then back on. I ignored it at first ‘cause it had happened before and I thought it was the radio- it being a POS and all.” Shortly after he explains how his lights would flicker he was losing engine power. So he flashed his friend and they all pulled over. “I opened the trunk and the engine was sparking! So I grabbed my fire extinguisher…” By the time the fire extinguisher was ready, Dj explains, the engine had caught fire! But it was quickly contained under Dj’s calmness and handling of situation. By that time is about 8:30-9pm and dark. “I didn’t have a flash light so I decided to leave it and come back on the morning and fix it. My car has been no stranger to the side of the road or a flat bed toe truck so I figured ‘what the hell it’s not like anyone is ganna steal it, its Florida, no-one stops’.” So he locked his car, jumped in with his friends in their vehicle and continued the trip.

    We all had a pretty good evening. Saw the movies, chatted, etc; you know the typical young adult stuff

   Not much after10am Sunday we (My roommate and I) were ready to go get his car. The only thing stopping him was his keys were missing. “ I was like ‘O, well, isn’t this convent!’”. I called the movie theater and couldn’t get a straight answer so my roommate went up to Orlando from Winter Haven to see them. But of course they did not find them.

  “I was kind of disappointed. They had four or five sets of keys but not mine. It’s not like someone else can actually USE some strangers keys.” Once out of the theater they headed to Dj’s car.

   “We pulled up behind it and I was hoping maybe I locked them inside. I could get into my car in and emergency if needed. It would have been detrimental to the car but I could get in.” But they didn’t need the keys, why? Because someone HAD stopped, not only stopped but they stole a large amount of Dj’s personal possessions from inside the car. “I have a good idea how they got in but it upsets me still.” According to estimates the thief’s stolen over a thousand dollars of merchandise. “…From my Pioneer CD deck to my POS CD player, to my 8 yrs. of CD’s, my black light, watch, half my tools, an damp I had gotten from a friend and rebuild from the inside out, and some other personal effects…”

   So what was your plan from there? No keys and your car torn up. “I figured I’d put an new ignition into it the next day and eat the loss. Not easy mind you but I’d have to.”

   The next day (Monday now) he returns about 1pm and makes his way into his car and spends the next 5 hours try into get a new ignition in it. “I like working on my car and I didn’t mind the time. I just kept getting pissed off because someone had done this to me. I don’t take thing’s like that lightly.”

   Around 6pm Dj surrendered the battle and decided to have it towed as soon as he had the funds available.

   It’s to bad he didn’t get it started that Monday evening.

   “Tuesday I was awakening to the phone. I answered and it was a friend from Haines City. He says: “ You know there’s no tires on your car right?”

I respond “Mmm no I didn’t know.”

Him: “Well when I passed it this morning I was laying cockeyed on the side of the road.”

Me: “Errrr (getting rather unset and angry). All right, thanks. (Attempting to calm down).”

    So not only did this guy have his car break down on I-4 on a Saturday night, he lost his keys, car was broken into early Sunday morning, AND had his tires stolen Monday! BUT wait there’s more!

    “I was sitting at work about 2pm Tuesday and this tow truck guy comes in and asks for me. Turns out the Highway Police called in an emergency tow because my car was sitting there with no tires. They traced the plates and tracked me down. So I got stuck with a $115 toe bill on top of what ever it will cost to replace to keys and ignition. We told them to bring the car by and we could park it were I was until I figure out what to do with it. But just like everything else that didn’t work out either. The tow company did not bring his car by. Wednesday Dj called them and ‘impolite’ reminded them they had what was left of his car. In response the company did bring by what was left, plus a $160 bill. “As one of my co-workers pointed out to me ‘they had me by the balls and there was nothing I could do about it.”

   What a thing to happen to a guy trying to save money to get into college and make something of his life.

   Dj has since then paid off his loans he made fro co-workers to get his car back and is planning to get his ‘parts car’ sometime within the next week. Sometimes the harder we try the more things go wrong until it seems like nothing else could happen. We never imagine it being this bad.
